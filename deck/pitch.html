The Problem

Finding a plumber in a Philippine town is still
word-of-mouth and crossed fingers.

💸 Payment risk

Pay ahead and hope? Or pay after and argue? Both sides lose. No escrow culture exists offline.

🫥 No verifiable trust

"My cousin knows a guy" is the entire verification system. No proof of work, no accountability.

📵 Digital exclusion

Existing platforms demand app installs, cards, and English fluency. The tricycle driver and his customers are locked out.

◆ SERBIZYU2 / 10
The Insight

Trust shouldn't be a promise.
It should be the architecture.

  • Escrow by default — every peso held in an internal ledger, released only on buyer confirmation (Xendit as rail, not source of truth)
  • Structured work — every booking follows a visible fulfillment contract with proof steps
  • Evidence over testimony — GPS logs, photos, sign-offs make disputes solvable in minutes
  • Meet users where they are — Messenger, SMS, mobile web. No install required to book or earn
  • Meet buyers where they scroll — listings travel to Facebook, TikTok, Google. Distribution is a product feature, not a marketing afterthought

Distribution — The Unfair Advantage

Listings that travel to the buyer,
not buyers we beg to come to us.

  • SSR public pages — every offer readable by Google, Facebook previews, and AI agents
  • One adapter per channel — the same integration that posts a listing also answers its replies (Facebook, Messenger, SMS, TikTok-ready)
  • Reciprocity engine — servicers share their free branded card; every share is a UTM-tagged acquisition channel
  • Human-approved automation — LLM drafts, curator publishes. ToS-safe by design
  • Channel ROI loop — attributed bookings feed the orchestrator's rotation weights
  • Target: 30%+ of bookings channel-attributed by Phase 3 — compounding reach competitors can't copy with ad spend

Business Model

We earn when trust converts.

  • Transaction fee — percentage per completed, escrow-released order (aligned with successful fulfillment, not listings)
  • Featured slots — performance-earned, visibility-priced rotation per town-category
  • Value-added services — disbursement convenience, asset generation boosts, coop dashboards (Phase 3)
  • Disciplined exclusions — no e-wallet, no Grab-clone, no licensed-profession liability. Focus is a feature.
Unit economics honesty:

Phase 1 exists to measure real take-rate tolerance in one town before we model national scale. We'd rather show you Candon's real numbers than a fictional TAM slide.
