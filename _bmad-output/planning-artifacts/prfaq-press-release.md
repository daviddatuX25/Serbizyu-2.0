# Serbizyu — PRFAQ (Working Backwards)

## Press Release

**Serbizyu Launches in Candon, Bringing 28 Service Categories Online for Ilocos Sur's Provincial Economy**

*CANDON, ILOCOS SUR — July 2026* — Serbizyu, the first inclusive services platform purpose-built for the Philippine provincial economy, today announced its launch in Candon City with 28 service categories spanning home repairs, beauty, agriculture, education, and transport.

Unlike existing platforms optimized for Metro Manila's digital-first users, Serbizyu was designed from the ground up for the 70% of provincial micro-entrepreneurs who own a feature phone but not a smartphone. Through its **Human Agent Network**, non-digital-literate business owners can list their services via a local agent who manages the digital presence while the owner retains full control via SMS-based consent.

**Key features at launch:**
- **28 categories** in 8 tiers covering 90% of provincial service needs
- **Quick Deal** — face-to-face transactions via QR code with escrow protection
- **Cash-first support** with dual-confirmation receipts and dispute protection
- **Escrow-backed payments** via Xendit with Shopee-style 3-day guarantee
- **Human Agent System** — trained local agents onboard offline businesses
- **10 fulfillment archetypes** supporting everything from a single haircut to a multi-month construction project
- **Deal-Chaining** — multi-slot, multi-servicer coordination under one deal container

## FAQ (Key Challenges Explored During Analysis)

**Q: Cost vs Trust — which is the primary barrier?**
A: The first barrier is the digital divide. The Human Agent Network solves this by letting non-digital-literate people work through agents. Trust is solved via escrow (Xendit) and evidence-tiered disputes. Cost is solved through tiered pricing, cash support, and subsidy overrides — keeping the barrier of entry low.

**Q: Does the agent layer become a bottleneck?**
A: No — it's a delegated access model. OTP/SMS consent ensures owner control. The graduation bonus (3× monthly commission) incentivizes agents to train owners toward independence, not trap them. Meesho's 190M+ user model proves this works at scale.

**Q: Can the economics work at provincial price points (₱50-₱500)?**
A: Yes — three-tier sliding scale (₱10 flat for micro, 8% capped at ₱150, 6% capped at ₱500), plus BMBE tax exemption for most provincial servicers. Cash transactions use a separate rate (12%) that includes dispute protection.

**Q: Why would servicers choose Serbizyu over Facebook Marketplace?**
A: Facebook has no transaction layer, no escrow, no trust infrastructure, and no quality assurance. Serbizyu provides booking management, escrow protection, dispute resolution, and distribution to multiple channels — not just a listing wall.
